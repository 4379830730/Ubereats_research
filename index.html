Finding 01 — Critical
Customer Support System Is Invisible

All three research methods independently converged: reaching a human agent in Uber Eats is architecturally impossible. No phone number exists anywhere in the app. The chatbot presents only chip options — no free-text box means typing "human" is impossible. Every path leads to deeper automation.

"it is really hard to find a human service to help. I did not finish the task eventually. AI makes it more difficult and longer time."

— PURE Evaluator 2, Task 3-5

"it feels like the app is hiding a phone number on purpose to force me to use the bot."

Finding 02 — New from PURE Study
Information Architecture Problems

The PURE expert evaluation identified IA as the core pain point driving cognitive overload — a finding absent from usability testing. While usability participants gave high SEQ scores for Task 1, PURE evaluators pinpointed why: the ordering interface lacks categorization logic, features are buried, and ads compete visually with key functions.

"another issue is hidden in the 'more option' choice which is also in a long list. I have to read all of that to only get started. that's frustrating."

— PURE Evaluator 2, Task 3

"The search bar is not on the top of the screen. It makes me confused a little bit. Then I find it on the bottom."

— PURE Evaluator 3, Task 1, Step 2

  • Search placement: Search bar at bottom nav violates H4 (Consistency). Category chip creates competing off-path affordance.
  • Menu hierarchy: Restaurant opens as bottom sheet with fee info first. Menu items (primary goal) buried below promo content.
  • Help page IA: "Another order issue" hidden in a long list inside "More Options." Violates recognition over recall (H6).
  • Visual hierarchy: Ads and key content have equal visual prominence. Interface perceived as cluttered marketing.
PURE vs Usability Comparison

Usability Testing showed high SEQ for Task 1 (6.2/7), masking IA problems. PURE corrected the assumption — the issue is not "too many ads" but a lack of visual hierarchy. PURE 2+ ratings on 13 of 17 steps confirm systemic friction throughout the entire app.

Finding 03 — Confirmed Both Studies
The Issue of Price Transparency

Both Usability Study and PURE Study independently flagged the same critical friction: users cannot verify the all-in total price before tapping "Place Order." Custom tip entry shows food + fees subtotal only ($17.51) — the tip is never added in the UI. Users must do mental arithmetic. The AI evaluation confirmed this, noting tip was calculated on pre-discount total ($26.51) rather than actual total ($17.51).

"I cannot see the total price after I adding a tip. That's a little bit annoying."

— Jane (Usability Participant, Task 2)

"When I reached the final step after selecting the tip, I couldn't find a summary of the total price anywhere. There was only a 'Place Order' button."

— PURE Evaluator 2, Task 2-7

Finding 04 — Medium Priority
Interface Prioritises Marketing Over Usability

Users find the interface cluttered because they must navigate extensive promotional content before reaching the restaurant menu. Upsell popups intercept the checkout flow with "No thanks" confirmshaming. The search bar sits at the bottom of the screen, buried below ads. AI and usability testing both flagged this; PURE experts did not encounter it — suggesting it affects real users more than expert walkthroughs.

Actionable Recommendations

Design Actions

Specific recommendations derived from all three research sources.

ACTION 01
Fix Customer Support Visibility
How might we establish a transparent communication mechanism that allows users to resolve their needs at any time?
  • Add a persistent floating Help icon on both home page and order page
  • Design 3-way communication platform: user ↔ merchant ↔ rider (ref: Meituan)
  • Expose human agent option prominently before AI chatbot, not after
  • Add free-text input to chatbot so users can type "human" or "agent"
KPI: NPS ↑ · Churn Rate ↓ · Support Cost ↓ · LTV ↑
ACTION 02
Redesign Information Architecture
How might we design an intuitive IA that minimises friction points for users during their interactions?
  • Move search bar from bottom nav to top of homepage
  • Add a visible left sidebar for restaurant menu categories
  • Redesign homepage: prioritize key functions, reduce ad visual weight
  • Use card sorting to restructure help page — separate order history from topic list
KPI: Cart Abandonment ↓ · Time-to-Order ↓ · Activation ↑
ACTION 03
Fix Price Transparency
How might we redesign the checkout flow to provide full price transparency and eliminate fear of price uncertainty?
  • Show all-in total (food + fees + tip) immediately after tip is set
  • Pin a running total to the bottom of checkout screen while scrolling
  • Add a payment confirmation dialog before "Place Order" to prevent misclicks
  • Calculate tip on actual discounted total, not pre-discount price
KPI: Order Completion ↑ · Refund Rate ↓ · CLTV ↑
